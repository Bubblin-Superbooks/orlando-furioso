LXXXVIII

A squire laced on her helmet, at her hest; She wore the remnant of her armour sheen; Nor without martial cuirass on her breast, Find I, that she ten times was ever seen, Even from the day when first that iron vest Braced on her limbs the passing-valiant queen: With helm on head, where, mid the highest rows, Brunello sits among the first, she goes.

LXXXIX

Him by mid breast Marphisa griped amain, And lifted up the losel from the ground; As is rapacious eagle wont to strain The pullet, in her talons circled round; And bore him where the sons of King Troyane Heard the two knights their jarring claims propound.
