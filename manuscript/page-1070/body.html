VI

Nor knowing where for shelter she should rove, Bradamant in that place resolves to stay, Couched on the verdant herbage of the grove; And, sleeping, now awaits the dawn of day, Now watching Saturn, Venus, Mars, and Jove, And the other wandering gods upon their way: But, whether waking or to sleep resigned, Has aye Rogero present to her mind.

VII

With sorrow and repentance oft assailed, She from her inmost heart profoundly sighed, That Anger over Love should have prevailed.
