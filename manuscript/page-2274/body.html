XLIV

"Not only Bradamant would I forego, But whatsoe'er I in the world possess; And rather forfeit life than ever know That grief, through me, should such a knight oppress. To me is thy distrust great cause of woe, That since thou couldst dispose of me no less Than of thyself, thou — rather than apply To me for succour — wouldst of sorrow die."

XLV
