CXXXVI

"Anselm, who saw no other wight beside To tell who was that mansion's lord, drew nigh To the Aethiopian, and to her applied; And she: `The owner of this house am I.' The judge was well assured the negress lied, And made that answer but in mockery: But with repeated oaths the negress swears; 'Tis hers, and none with her the mansions shares;

CXXXVII

"And would he see the palace, him invites To view it at his ease; and recommends If there be ought within which him delights, To take it for himself or for his friends. Anselmo hears, and from his horse alights, Gives it his man; and o'er the threshold wends; And by the hag conducted, mounts from hall Below to bower
