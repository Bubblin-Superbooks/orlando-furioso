CXV

"Knowing my sin, I ask not, I, to be — Such grace I dare not ask — restored to light; For well I ween such power resides in thee, As Being accepted in thy Maker's sight. Let it suffice, that I no longer see, Nor let me with perpetual hunger fight. At least, expel the harpies' loathsome horde, Nor let them more pollute my ravaged board;

CXVI

"And I to build thee, in my royal hold, A holy temple, made of marble, swear, With all its portals and its roof of gold, And decked, within and out, with jewels rare.
