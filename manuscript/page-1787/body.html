XXVI

They rode an-end; and louder waxed the sound, And plainer were the plaintive words they heard: When in a valley they three women found Making that plaint, who in strange garb appeared: For to the navel were those three ungowned, — Their coats by some uncourteous varlet sheared — And knowing not how better to disguise Their shame, they sate on earth, and dared not rise.
