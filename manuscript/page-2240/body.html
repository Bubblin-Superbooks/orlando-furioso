LXXXVIII

"Yet was but to myself this injury done, Myself to spare (because this touched but me) I haply could, yet hardly could, be won; Nay, I will say outright, I could not be. Less can I be, since not to me alone, But Bradamant, is done this injury; Even if I could consent myself to spare, It fits me not unvenged to leave that fair.

LXXXIX

"Then I the damsel will avenge, and die, (Nor this disturbs me whatsoe'er betide; )
