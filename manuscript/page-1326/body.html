Fury and scorn Gradasso's visage heats, Which seems to flash with fire, at that disgrace; And with more rage and pain his bosom beats, In that 'twas offered in such public place. To draw his scimeter, the king retreats, Intent upon revenge, some little space. So Mandricardo on himself relies Rogero he to fight, as well defies.

"Come on in arms against me, both combined, And be King Rodomont the third!" (he said) "Come Spain and Afric and all human kind; Ne'er will I turn." And he, at nought dismaid, So saying, in his fury, sawed the wind About him, with Almontes' noble blade, Embraced his shield, and, full of choler, stood Against Gradasso and Rogero good.
