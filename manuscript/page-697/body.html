LXVIII

Well matched awhile the Christian and the Moor Appeared, without advantage in the fray. Not this, now that gave ground, like corn before The light and fickle breeze which blows in May: Or as the sea which ripples on the shore, Still comes and goes, nor keeps one certain way, When hollow Fortune thus had sported long, She proved disastrous to the paynim throng.
