XXVIII

Minded to take the hippogryph, he flung The saddle on him, which lay near, and bitted The steed, by choosing, all the reins among, This part or that, until his mouth was fitted: For in that place were many bridles hung, Belonging to the coursers which flitted. And now alone, intent upon his flight, The thought of Rabicane detained the knight.
