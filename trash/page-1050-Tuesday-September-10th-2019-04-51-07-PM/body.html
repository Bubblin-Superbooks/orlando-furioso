The Child demanded of that elder, who Was he that from the gate first took his way, And he, " 'Tis Sansonet; of crimson hue, I know his surcoat, with white flowers gay." Without a word exchanged, the warlike two Divide the ground, and short is the delay. For they against each other, levelling low Their spears, and hurrying sore their coursers, go.

LXV

This while had issued from the fortress near, With many footmen girt, Sir Pinnabel, All ready to despoil the cavalier, Who in the warlike joust should void is sell. At one another spurred in bold career The knights, with their huge lances rested well. Up to the points nigh equal was each stick, Of stubborn native oak, and two palms thick.
