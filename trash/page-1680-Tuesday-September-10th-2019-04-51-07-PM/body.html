Continuing his discourse, that elder hoar Raised mighty wonder in the cavalier, When he avouched, as he his name disclosed, That he THE HOLY GOSPEL, had composed;

LVIII

He of our Lord so loved, the blessed John; Of whom a speech among the brethren went, He never should see death, and hence the Son Of God with this rebuke St. Peter shent; In saying, "What is it to thee, if one Tarry on earth, till I anew be sent?" Albeit he said not that he should not die, That so he meant to say we plain descry.
