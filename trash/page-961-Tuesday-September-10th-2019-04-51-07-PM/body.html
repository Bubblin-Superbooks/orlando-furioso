XCVII

The duke arrives, seeking the friendly band, Whom he had hoped to find upon the quay; He turns and gazes round the desert strand, And none is there — directs along the bay His eyes, and now, far distant from the land, Beholds the parting frigate under way. So that the paladin, for his escape — The vessel gone — must other project shape.
