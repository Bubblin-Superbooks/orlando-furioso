LXXXVI

Through wood and field his courser did he goad, Often inquiring for the royal dame: Beside himself, he strayed beside his road, And to the foot of rising mountain came, Whence (it was night-time through a fissure glowed )

The distant flicker of a quivering flame. Orlando to the rock approached, to spy If there Angelica concealed might lie.
