XXX

Henceforth with that apostle let the peer Remain; for I have now to make a spring As far as 'tis from heaven to earth; for here I cannot hang for ever on the wing. I to the dame return, who was whilere Wounded by jealousy with cruel sting. I left her where, successively o'erthrown, Three kings she quickly upon earth had strown;

XXXII

And afterwards arriving in a town, At eve, which on the road to Paris lay, Heard tidings of Rinaldo's victory
