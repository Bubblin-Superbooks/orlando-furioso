XIX

Two crystal streams the wealthy city scower; Whose currents, parted into many a rill, Infinite gardens, never bare of flower, Or stript of leaf, with grateful murmur fill: 'Tis said the perfumed waters are of power (So plenteously they swell to turn a mill; )

And that whoever wander through the streets, Scent, issuing from each home, a cloud of sweets.
