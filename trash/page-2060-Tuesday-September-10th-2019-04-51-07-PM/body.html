VI

Fain would he think awhile, of whom I speak, (As said ere to his lips the vase he bore; )

He thought; then thus: "When finding what we seek Displeases, this 'tis folly to explore, My wife's a woman; every woman's weak. Then let me hold the faith I held before. Faith still has brought, and yet contentment brings. From proof itself what better profit springs?

VII

"From this small good, much evil I foresee: For tempting God moves sometimes his disdain. I know not if it wise or foolish be, But to know more than needs, I am not fain.
