CXX

"Why has not bounteous Nature willed that man Should be produced without the aid of thee, As we the pippin, pear, and service can Engraft by art on one another's tree? But she directs not all by certain plan; Rather, upon a nearer view, I see, In naming her, she ill can act aright, Since Nature is herself a female hight.

CXXI

"Yet be not therefore proud and full of scorn Women, because man issues from your seed; For roses also blossom on the thorn, And the fair lily springs from loathsome weed. Despiteous, proud, importunate, and lorn Of love, of faith, of counsel, rash in deed, With that, ungrateful, cruel and perverse, And born to be the world's eternal curse!"
