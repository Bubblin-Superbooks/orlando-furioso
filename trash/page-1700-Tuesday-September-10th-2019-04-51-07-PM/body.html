VI

"Between the king of rivers' horns," (he cries,) "Stands what is now a small and humble town. Before it runs the Po, behind it lies A misty pool of marsh; this — looking down The stream of future years — I recognize First of Italian cities of renown; Not only famed for wall and palace rare, But noble ways of life and studies fair.

VII

"Such exaltation, reached so suddenly, Is not fortuitous nor wrought in vain; But that is may his worthy cradle be, Whereof I speak, shall so the heaven ordain.
