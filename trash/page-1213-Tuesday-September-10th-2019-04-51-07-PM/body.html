"Then, ordering from her store a costly vest, She spread it, and — as I a woman were — The lady me in that rich garment drest, And in a golden net confined my hair. I gravely moved my eye-balls, nor confest, By gesture or by look, the sex I bear. My voice, which might discover the deceit, I tuned so well that none perceived the cheat.

LVI

"Next to the hall, where dame and cavalier In crowds are gathered, we united go; Who make to us such court and goodly cheer, As men to queen or high-born lady show. Here oft I laughed at some, with secret jeer, Who, knowing not the sex concealed below My flowing robe of feminine array, Wooed me with wishful eyes in wanton way.
