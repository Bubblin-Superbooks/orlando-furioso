XLVII

"I with dishonour life to flight may owe; But worse than death loath thus to save my head. The worst that can befall me if I go, Is I my blood shall with the others shed: But if on me such mercy God bestow, That I remain alive, the giant dead, Secure for thousands shall I make the ways; So that the greater good the risque o'erpays.

XLVIII

"I peril but the single life of one Against safety of the countless rest." — "Go then in peace," (the other said). "my son, And to thy succour, form among the blest, May God dispatch the Archangel Michael down." — And him, with that, the simple hermit blest. Astolpho
